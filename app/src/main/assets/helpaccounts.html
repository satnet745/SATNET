Accounts and identity

SATNET uses an Android account plus local Serval/SATNET identity data to provide initial identity management. A unique SID is generated for the device during setup and remains the durable mesh identity for that installation.
Your display name and phone number or alias can be changed later, but they do not replace the underlying SID. If you need a completely new underlying identity, you generally need to reset or reinstall the app and repeat setup.
SATNET financial roles such as user, agent, merchant, and verifier are separate from the Android account. Those roles are configured from the SATNET Bitcoin flow, not from the Contacts screen.

Contacts

The Contacts area helps you view nearby peers and link them into the Android contact store when useful. A peer is any participant currently reachable on the SATNET mesh.
If no other phones appear, open Connect, make sure SATNET services are enabled, and check whether another reachable peer is visible on Wi-Fi or Bluetooth.
SATNET can add contacts to a dedicated area of the standard Android address book. Depending on the handset, you may need to enable the SATNET account source in Android contact display options before those entries appear consistently.
If a peer changes their name or number, Android contact entries may need to be refreshed manually if they were previously imported into the phone's contact store.
