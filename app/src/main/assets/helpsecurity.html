SATNET is designed around local-first communication, user-controlled identities, and self-custodial Bitcoin tooling. Calls, messages, contacts, file sharing, and SATNET wallet flows all depend on the security of the device you are holding.
Protect the phone itself with a strong screen lock, keep the operating system updated, and do not install untrusted software on the same device.
If you use SATNET wallet features, store the recovery phrase offline in a safe place. Do not leave it in screenshots, cloud notes, or long-lived clipboard history. Panic mode and hidden-balance UI reduce immediate shoulder-surfing risk, but they do not replace careful recovery-phrase handling.
SATNET Maps is also designed to minimize exposure: temporary locations stay local unless you save them, and saved bookmarks are encrypted on-device before optional Rhizome export.
We also provide a list of all Android permissions used by SATNET and what they are used for.
