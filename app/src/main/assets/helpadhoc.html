Ad Hoc Wi-Fi is legacy and experimental

Ad hoc Wi-Fi is not a normal, modern Android networking path. It remains in SATNET mainly for older device experiments, historical compatibility testing, and specialist deployments that understand the risks.
On many devices it requires root access, custom Wi-Fi driver behavior, or firmware combinations that Android does not officially support.
Because of that, ad hoc tests can fail, leave networking in a broken state until reboot, or simply be unavailable on your hardware.
For most current SATNET use, prefer standard Wi-Fi, portable hotspot mode, and Bluetooth-assisted peer discovery from the Connect screen.
If you deliberately use mesh testing, do it only on a device you can recover and only after understanding that the feature is for advanced compatibility testing rather than normal daily use.
