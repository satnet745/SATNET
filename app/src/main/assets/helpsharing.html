Sharing

SATNET includes built-in distributed file sharing through Rhizome.
You can share messages, photos, documents, and other files over the mesh. SATNET Maps also includes a separate flow for exporting encrypted bookmark bundles over Rhizome after you choose a shared passphrase.
Shared files consume device storage. On older devices this may rely on external storage; on newer devices it may use app-managed storage exposed through Android's storage model. You remain responsible for available space.
Rhizome file sharing should be treated as distributed storage, not as a private encrypted messenger. Only share material you are prepared to distribute to other participating devices.

Sharing Files

To share files, open Share Files from the main screen. Current Rhizome flows let you publish files, browse shared content, and view saved items.
SharePublish files onto the SATNET mesh.
FindBrowse content available from nearby peers.
SavedView files that have already been downloaded or preserved locally.

Sharing the app

You can also share the SATNET app with someone even without depending on an app store.
Open Share SATNET from the main interface. The flow can guide nearby recipients to the APK over local connectivity and may also let you use other Android sharing methods available on the device.
If the sharing flow asks you to start a hotspot or join a local SSID, do that from the normal Android network controls or the Connect screen, then return to the sharing flow.
Alternatively, use any safe local sharing method available on the phone, such as Bluetooth or Android's built-in share sheet.
Please be sure you only share this software if you have obtained it from a trusted software source- if not, it may contain malicious software that could damage your handset, or infect it with a virus.
